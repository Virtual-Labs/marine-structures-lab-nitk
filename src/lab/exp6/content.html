Fineness modulus of cement
Theory

Cements exhibit different properties and characteristics depending upon their chemical compositions. By changing the fineness of grinding or the oxide composition, cement can be made to exhibit different properties.


The fineness of cement has an important bearing on the rate of hydration and hence on the rate of gain of strength and also on the rate of evolution of heat. Finer cement offers a greater surface area for hydration and hence faster the development of strength. Different cements are ground to different fineness. The fineness of cement is measured by sieving it through a standard sieve. The disadvantage of fine grinding is that it is susceptible to air-set and early deterioration. Maximum number of particles in a sample of cement should have a size less than about 100 microns. The smallest particle may have a size of about 1.5 microns. By and large an average size of the cement particles may be taken as about 10 micron. The particle size fraction below 3 microns has been found to have the predominant effect on the strength at one day while 3-25 micron fraction has a major influence on the 28 days strength. Increase in fineness of cement is also found to increase the drying shrinkage of concrete. In commercial cement it is suggested that there should be about 25-30 percent of particles of less than 7 micron in size.


The proportion of cement grain larger than the specified mesh size is determined by sieve analysis. i.e., the percentage weight retained on the sieve


% weight retained on the sieve=Weight of residue / Total weight of the sample


Precautions to be taken while performing sieve analysis:


1. The cement sample should not have any air lumps.


2. Sieve should be done by rotating the sieve not by translation.


Relevant Indian Standard: IS:4031(Part 1):1996 - Method of physical test for cement (Determination of fineness by dry sieving).
